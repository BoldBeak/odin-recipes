Pan Fried Pork Chops


Description

Quick and easy pan fried pork chops.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless pork chops about 1/2 inch thick
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon butter or margerine
  • 2 teaspoons of dried rosemary
  • salt (to taste)
  • black pepper (to taste)

Sauce (optional)

  • 2 teaspoons corn starch
  • 1/2 to 1c hot water
  • 1 cup additional liquid (water, broth, white wine, etc.)
  • salt (to taste)
  • pepper (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Remove pork chops from packaging and pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Preheat frying pan on medium heat.
  3. Salt both sides of chops liberally and sprinkle with pepper to taste.
  4. Add oil and butter or margerine to the pan.
  5. Crush rosemary leaves then sprinkle evenly over both sides of chops.
  6. Add all 4 chops to the pan.
  7. Cook for about 6 minutes on each side or until middle reaches 165 degrees Farenheit.
  8. Reompve from pan and allow to rest about 5 minutes before serving.

Steps for optional Sauce:

Tip! Get everything for your sauce ready while your chops are cooking.

  1. In a small bowl or cup, gradually add hot water to cornstarch until it is completely dissolved.
    Set aside.
  2. Immediately after removing chops from the pan, add the 1 cup of additional liquid of your choice.
  3. Use a whisk or other utensil safe for your pan to scrape up anything stuck to the bottom of the pan.
  4. Bring the liquid to a slow boil then add the cornstarch slurry.
  5. Continue to sitr the mixture as it simmers until it thickens slightly.
  6. Remove from heat and allow to cool for about 5 minutes before serving.
  7. Serve over chops, mashed potatoes, or anything else you'd like.
